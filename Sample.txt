Fire Alarm System Project

1. Introduction A fire alarm system is a safety device used to detect
fire hazards such as smoke, heat, or gas and alert people through
alarms. It helps prevent accidents and saves lives by giving early
warning.

2. Problem Statement Fire accidents in homes, industries, and public
places can cause severe damage to life and property. Many times,
fires are detected too late due to lack of proper monitoring
systems.

3. Proposed Solution To design a smart fire alarm system using sensors
and microcontrollers that can detect smoke, temperature rise, or gas
leakage and alert users immediately using a buzzer or mobile
notification.

4. Components Used

- Arduino Uno / NodeMCU – Main controller
- MQ-2 Smoke Sensor – Detects smoke and gas
- DHT11 Sensor – Measures temperature and humidity
- Buzzer – Produces alarm sound
- LED – Visual alert
- Relay Module – Controls external devices
- Power Supply
- Blynk App (optional)

5. Working Principle The MQ-2 sensor detects smoke or gas in the
environment. The DHT11 sensor monitors temperature changes. If smoke
level or temperature exceeds a threshold, the buzzer turns on, LED
glows, and notification is sent to mobile. Relay can activate safety
systems.

6. Key Features

- Early fire detection
- Real-time alerts
- Low cost and easy installation
- IoT-based monitoring
- Automatic response

7. Product Overview This system is a compact and efficient fire safety
solution designed for homes, offices, and industries.

8. Advantages

- Prevents fire accidents
- Saves lives and property
- Easy to operate
- Low maintenance

9. Applications Homes, industries, schools, hospitals, and warehouses.

10. Limitations

- Possible false alarms
- Needs power supply
- Limited detection range

11. Future Enhancements

- AI-based prediction
- GSM alerts
- Automatic extinguishing system

12. Conclusion The fire alarm system helps in early detection and
prevention of fire accidents and improves safety effectively.
